Wessex WebSDR

This WebSDR is located in the South West UK.

50-52MHz

The 6m band uses a Tri-Band Colinear mounted at 20m AGL

  • This WebSDR can be used to listen to 145.800MHz FM
    • Track the current location and radio footprint of the ISS at the ARISS/ESA Map
    • You may be able to find a WebSDR nearer you to listen to the ISS on the websdr.org index
    • Watch ESA's instructional videos on how to configure your PC/Mac/Raspberry Pi to receive SSTV: ESA Education
144-146MHz

The VHF band uses a Tri-Band Colinear mounted at 20m AGL

  • This WebSDR can be used to listen to live transmissions from the International Space Station, including SSTV on 145.800MHz FM
    • Track the current location and radio footprint of the ISS at the ARISS/ESA Map
    • You may be able to find a WebSDR nearer you to listen to the ISS on the websdr.org index
    • Watch ESA's instructional videos on how to configure your PC/Mac/Raspberry Pi to receive SSTV: ESA Education
432-434MHz

The 70cms band uses a Tri-Band Colinear mounted at 20m AGL.

  • Many CubeSats and other satellites can be heard on this band
  • More information about active amateur spacecraft can be found at https://www.amsat.org/status/
  • It is planned to add additional SDR hardware to receive the 432-435 MHz part of the band in the future.
10GHz

The 3cms system uses a Alford Slot antenna feeding an Octagon LNB.

  • The main purpose of this SDR is to monitor for tropo propagation to the south of the UK
  • GB3SCX, the Bell Hill 3cms beacon, can be heard on 10368.905MHz (actually 1296.856MHz in September 2022).

The system uses a .

  • Like the 3cm system the 13cm system is to monitor tropo propagation to the south of the UK
  • Under normal conditions no beacons are within range, the nearest is GB3SCK, at Bell Hill, on 24048.905MHz 2320.905MHz.
